Moodboard

(1) I used a blend of greens and a light brown to encapsulate an earthy theme, this was done to suit my fern based software solution, as the earthy tone replicates that of a fern colours albeit less saturated to ease readability. This is also the reason for the accompanying white, which is to be used for text ensuring it stands out. This contrast was important for navigability, something that I had to keep in mind for my varied tech expertise amongst users, as poor navigability would hurt my lower technical experience user class.

(2) My font choice, Varela Round, was chosen due to its well-defined and readable style while giving an "organic" feel due to its soft terminals and open counters when combined with the earthy theme established in my colour palette. It was the perfect blend of readability while fitting my earthy fern theme, ensuring the solution remains navigable while aesthetically suited.

Mind Map

I chose to split my mindmap into four main branches: appearance and usability, fern identification, mis-identification reports and developer tools.

(1) Appearance and usability is where I branched off further to detail my earthy theme with shades of green and brown while using white for text. This was done to demonstrate how the colour palette of my software solution would be match a fern's typical colour albeit less saturated to enhance readability and navigation. Furthermore I branched ideas of affordance for my users with less technical experience as my survey foundings reported a varied technical expertise.

(2) Fern identification is where I branched off to explain how the fern identification process would work functionally and its appearance, including how a fern can be identified by a fern key questionnaire or photo recognition, further branching to include the processes behind them such as the fern key sorting algorithm or machine learning required. Elsewise I also included branches of location verification and what information is provided about the fern being identified, this created the framework for what my fern identification feature could look like.

(3) Mis-identification reports is where I branched off to discuss the functionality of such a feature, including how the user makes one and what information is included, otherwise the aspect of how the developer reads them. This gave me a list of pontential data that would be included in the sending of the mis-identification reports.

(4) Finally developer tools is where I branched off to visualise what kind of developer tools would exist, their functionality and how they would be integrated, providing myself a blueprint of tools that could be implemented into my design tools.
